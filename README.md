Retail pricing today is mostly static. A jacket gets a price tag when it hits the store, and that price barely changes unless there's a sale. But in reality, the value of that product changes constantly based on where it's placed, what season it is, whether it's being promoted, and how well it's selling.

Zara loses money because of this. A leather jacket sitting in the aisle during peak winter season is underpriced. The same jacket on an end-cap with a promotion running is probably being discounted unnecessarily. Nobody is optimizing these decisions with data.

"It's a what-if engine for pricing, tell it your store conditions, it tells you what to charge."
